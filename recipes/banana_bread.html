Yummy Banana Bread

Back to recipebook

Description:

Do you always find those last couple of bananas you bought last week getting too ripe to eat then thrown away?

If you do, then boy do i have a recipe for you, and if you dont? Well that shouldnt stop you from trying this recipe.

Following every step should reward you with a nice and tasty loaf of banana bread with a nice and soft inside, and a crisp, sweet crust.

Ingredients:

  • One cup sugar
  • Two cups flower
  • One and a half teaspoons baking powder
  • One fourth teaspoon salt
  • Half a cup vegetable oil
  • Three teaspoons vanilla extract
  • Three eggs
  • Three very ripe bananas
  • Can of spray oil
  • [optional] A tablespoon of cinnamon
  • [optional] Half a cup chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. Get all of your ingredients out, along with two large bowls and a small cake pan
  2. Begin pouring the sugar, flower, and all the other dry ingredients into one bowl
  3. Do the same for the other bowl with the bananas, eggs and the rest of the ingredients.
  4. With a rubber spatula, or whisk, begin to break apart the bananas and mix it together with the rest of the ingredients.
  5. Once you've thouroghly mixed the wet ingredients together, pour them out into the bowl of dry ingredients.
  6. Mix well until its all a thick runny dough. If it becomes too hard to mix add a small bit of water and continue mixing.
  7. Now, spray the whole surface inside of the cake pan and pour the batter into it.
  8. Set the oven to 350F and put the pan in on the middle tray
  9. Let cook for roughly 50 minutes. After 50 minutes check if the inside is done by poking a toothpick into the top of the loaf

    10. If dough is stuck to the toothpick then leave the loaf in the oven and repeat in two minutes untill the toothpick comes out dry

  10. Let the pan rest on your stovetop for 10 minutes, then enjoy!

Storing Instructions

There are 3 different ways you can store this bread.

  1. You put plastic wrap snuggly over the top and poke in a few holes with a toothpick. Store on your countertop or in your cabinet.

    2. -This will soften the crust of the bread while keeping the whole thing slightly moist, and more importantly, yummy.

  2. Put a kitchen towel over the top and rest on countertop or in cabinet.

    3. -This will keep the crust of the bread from getting softer but will also dry out the rest of the loaf.

  3. Lastly, put tin foil over the top.

    4. -This keeps the crust the crispest while also keeping the rest of the loaf moist.
